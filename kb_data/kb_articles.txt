Microphone not working in meetings
Your microphone may not be detected due to incorrect input settings. In Windows, go to Settings > Sound > Volume and input settings. Check that the correct microphone is selected as the default device. If the microphone is muted in the system tray, unmute it. In your video conferencing application, verify that the correct microphone is selected in the app's audio settings. If using a USB microphone, try a different USB port. Restart the application. Check if another application is using the microphone exclusively.

Bluetooth device keeps disconnecting
Bluetooth devices disconnect due to interference or pairing issues. Move the Bluetooth device closer to your computer, away from other wireless devices like routers or microwaves. Restart Bluetooth on your computer: go to Settings > Devices > Bluetooth and toggle it off for 10 seconds, then back on. Remove the pairing and re-pair the device. Update Bluetooth drivers from Device Manager. Check the device's battery level. In Bluetooth settings, check for any firmware updates for the device.

---
Internet speed is very slow
Slow internet speed can have multiple causes. Run a speed test using speedtest.net to measure actual vs expected speeds. Restart your modem and router by unplugging both for 30 seconds. Check if other devices on the network are consuming bandwidth. Move closer to the router to improve signal strength. Scan for malware that might be consuming bandwidth. Contact your ISP to check for outages or line issues. Replace old Ethernet cables with Cat5e or Cat6 cables for better performance.

---
USB port not recognized
A USB device may not be detected if the port is faulty or drivers are missing. Try connecting the device to a different USB port. If it works on another port, that port may be broken. Restart your computer to reset USB drivers. Update USB drivers from Device Manager: locate the USB hub, right-click and select Update Driver. Disable USB Selective Suspend in Power Options to prevent the port from turning off. If using a USB hub, try connecting directly to the computer instead.

---
Video freezing during calls
Video freezes are typically caused by network issues or insufficient bandwidth. Check your internet connection speed and stability. Close other applications consuming bandwidth like downloads or streaming services. Move closer to your router to improve connection quality. In your video conferencing app, lower the video quality or resolution. Disable video background effects. Restart the video conferencing application. Update your graphics card drivers. Check if the app itself has a memory leak and needs updating.
